Beyond the catalog

What if my customer wants something not in your catalog?

A four-stage request pipeline that captures custom demand, applies layered markups automatically, and lands a complete quote in the agency's inbox — often in under 90 seconds.

01 / 12
The problem

Curated catalogs only solve 60% of agency demand.

China is vast, traveler preferences are infinite, and even our best six packages only fit the typical case. What about a 16-person corporate incentive across three countries? A celebrity honeymoon at La Mamounia in a presidential suite? A government delegation that needs the Aqaba Free Zone tour? The catalog will never cover these — but the value-add must.

Without a custom pipeline

Wholesaler picks up the phone, calls the supplier, negotiates outside the system. Margin gets squeezed, Safasoft DMC sees nothing, agency waits days.

The single-hop alternative

Today's basic RFQ ends at the wholesaler. Works for "swap one hotel" — breaks down when the request needs real supplier input.

The four-stage pipeline

Agency → wholesaler → Safasoft DMC → supplier, then the supplier quote returns with markups composed automatically at each layer. End-to-end inside the system.

Three benefits — captures demand outside the catalog · margins stack cleanly with no downstream negotiation · configurable speed from full auto to full manual.

The pipeline

Forward to the supplier. Back to the agency with margin stacked.

A single record — the CustomRequest — moves through four parties in two legs. The forward leg routes the brief downstream until a supplier quotes. The return leg adds markup at each layer on the way back. The agency only ever sees the final retail.

The rules engine

One record. Priority-ordered. First match wins.

A markup rule is a single record with a scope (Safasoft DMC or wholesaler), a set of matchers (any of: country, theme, hotel tier, supplier net thresholds, agency tier, etc.), a markup formula, and two flags — auto_apply and auto_forward. Rules sort by priority; the evaluator returns the first match. No second match, no fallback chain.
